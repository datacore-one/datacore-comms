# Tribal Marketing Playbook

Strategic guide based on Seth Godin's frameworks for building communities and spreading ideas.

## Core Principles

### 1. Tribes (Community Building)

**Definition**: A tribe = shared interest + means to communicate + leader

**Three Steps to Build a Tribe**:
1. **Desire to change** - Have a vision worth following
2. **Ability to connect** - Give people ways to talk to each other
3. **Willingness to lead** - Step up with authenticity

**Key Insights**:
- Size doesn't matter: small motivated group > large weak connection
- Leaders don't need titles - they need authenticity and commitment
- Create tribes that create tribes (viral community growth)
- The connection economy: influence built on relationships, not resources

### 2. Purple Cow (Being Remarkable)

**Definition**: A Purple Cow is something "worth making a remark about"

**Core Principles**:
- Be remarkable or be invisible
- Purple Cow is built-in, not slapped on (inherent to product/service)
- Stop advertising, start innovating
- Target a niche first, then expand
- There's no shortage of remarkable ideas - there's a shortage of will to execute

**The Test**: Would someone voluntarily tell others about this?

### 3. Permission Marketing

**Interruption vs Permission**:
- **Interruption**: Ads, cold calls, spam - unwanted attention
- **Permission**: Earned right to communicate with people who WANT to hear

**How to Earn Permission**:
1. Be remarkable first (Purple Cow)
2. Provide value consistently
3. Build trust over time
4. Only then ask for attention/action

### 4. Ideavirus Mechanics

**How Ideas Spread**:
- Customer-to-customer > business-to-customer
- Give away your best stuff for free - it spreads

**Key Components**:

| Component | Definition | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Sneezers** | Key spreading agents | Influencers, super-fans |
| **Hive** | Targeted audience | Your tribe |
| **Vector** | Path the idea travels | Social media, word of mouth |
| **Velocity** | Speed of spread | Viral vs slow burn |

**Two Types of Sneezers**:
1. **Promiscuous Sneezers**: Talkative, share with everyone, lower influence per share
2. **Powerful Sneezers**: Highly influential, respected in hive, higher impact per share

### 5. Smallest Viable Audience

**Principle**: Stop chasing mass, focus on better

**Why It Works**:
- Find smallest possible market
- Solve their problem so well they tell friends
- Easier to become essential to 1000 than invisible to 1M
- Quality relationships > quantity of followers

**1000 True Fans** (Kevin Kelly):
- 1000 people who will buy anything you make
- They provide feedback, become evangelists
- The foundation for sustainable growth

## Tribe Building Workflow

### Phase 1: Define Your Tribe

**Questions to Answer**:
1. Who are the 1000 true fans?
2. What shared interest binds them?
3. What change do they want to see?
4. Where do they currently gather?
5. How can they communicate with each other?

**Tribe Definition Template**:
```markdown
## Tribe Profile: [Name]

**Shared Interest**: [What binds them]
**Desired Change**: [What they want to happen]
**Current Frustration**: [What's blocking them]
**Where They Gather**: [Platforms, communities]
**How They Identify**: [Language, signals, badges]
```

### Phase 2: Find Your Sneezers

**Identify Both Types**:

**Promiscuous Sneezers**:
- Active on social media
- Share frequently
- Love being first to know
- Wide but shallow reach

**Powerful Sneezers**:
- Respected authorities
- Selective about what they share
- Deep influence in niche
- Worth extra effort to reach

**Sneezer Mapping**:
```markdown
## Sneezer List

### Powerful Sneezers (5-10)
| Name | Platform | Reach | How to Reach |
|------|----------|-------|--------------|
| [Name] | [X/LinkedIn] | [Followers] | [Strategy] |

### Promiscuous Sneezers (20-50)
| Name | Platform | Engagement | Notes |
|------|----------|------------|-------|
| [Name] | [Platform] | [Rate] | [Interests] |
```

### Phase 3: Create Remarkable Content

**Purple Cow Test** (must pass all):
- [ ] Would someone voluntarily tell others about this?
- [ ] Does this challenge conventional wisdom?
- [ ] Is this built-in, not marketing slapped on?
- [ ] Would a sneezer stake their reputation sharing this?

**Sneezer Test**:
- [ ] Does this make the sharer look smart/informed?
- [ ] Is it easy to share (quote, retweet, screenshot)?
- [ ] Does it provide exclusive/insider knowledge?
- [ ] Would powerful sneezers risk their credibility?

**Tribe Test**:
- [ ] Does this strengthen tribe identity?
- [ ] Does this speak to the shared interest?
- [ ] Does this move toward the desired change?
- [ ] Does this differentiate us from outsiders?

### Phase 4: Earn Permission

**Permission Ladder**:
```
STRANGER
  ↓ Remarkable content (free value)
AWARE
  ↓ Consistent value over time
INTERESTED
  ↓ Direct engagement, responses
CONNECTED
  ↓ Opted-in to communication
PERMITTED
  ↓ Trust established
ADVOCATE (Sneezer)
```

**Rules for Permission**:
1. Never interrupt - always provide value first
2. Make opting-in easy and opting-out easier
3. Respect the privilege - don't abuse frequency
4. Every communication should be worth their time

## Integration with Other Frameworks

### With STEPPS (Jonah Berger)

| STEPPS Element | Tribal Application |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Social Currency | Sneezers need to look smart sharing |
| Triggers | Associate with tribe's common experiences |
| Emotion | Tap into tribe's shared frustrations/aspirations |
| Public | Create visible tribe identity (hashtags, badges) |
| Practical Value | Solve tribe's specific problems |
| Stories | Tell the tribe's origin story and victories |

### With Content Matrix

| Content Type | Tribal Purpose |
|--------------|----------------|
| Actionable | Help tribe members succeed |
| Motivational | Celebrate tribe victories |
| Analytical | Explain why tribe's approach works |
| Contrarian | Differentiate from outsiders |
| Observation | Show tribe's worldview is right |
| Comparison | Prove tribe's choice is best |
| Future-Focused | Paint vision tribe is building toward |
| Listicle | Curate resources for tribe |

### With StoryBrand

| StoryBrand Element | Tribal Application |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Hero | Tribe member, not the brand |
| Problem | Tribe's shared frustration |
| Guide | Brand with empathy + authority |
| Plan | Path to tribe's desired change |
| Call to Action | Join the tribe, take first step |
| Failure | What happens if tribe fails |
| Success | The world tribe is building |

## Measurement

### Tribe Health Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| Member-to-member engagement | Tribe talking to each other | Growing |
| Sneezer share rate | Influential shares | 1+ per post |
| Permission growth | Opted-in members | 10%+ monthly |
| Return engagement | Same people engaging | 30%+ |
| Tribe language adoption | Using your terms | Spreading |

### Sneezer Effectiveness

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Sneezer shares | How often they share your content |
| Second-order spread | Their followers resharing |
| Conversion from sneezer reach | Actions from their audience |
| Sneezer retention | Do they keep sharing over time? |

## Quick Reference

### The Tribal Marketing Checklist

**Before Creating Content**:
- [ ] Who is this for? (Smallest viable audience)
- [ ] What change does the tribe want?
- [ ] Will sneezers share this?
- [ ] Is this remarkable (Purple Cow)?

**Before Publishing**:
- [ ] Does this strengthen tribe identity?
- [ ] Does it provide permission-worthy value?
- [ ] Is it easy for sneezers to spread?
- [ ] Does it move toward the desired change?

**After Publishing**:
- [ ] Did sneezers engage?
- [ ] Are tribe members talking to each other?
- [ ] Did we earn more permission?
- [ ] What made this remarkable (or not)?
